The week's structural story is infrastructure and IP at the same time: OpenAI's first custom chip shrinks its dependence on Nvidia while Anthropic's Senate allegations against Alibaba formalize model distillation as a national-security flashpoint. Epoch AI's quiet audit of FrontierMath — finding errors in nearly half the original problems — rewrites what every frontier math leaderboard claim of the past year actually meant.

## <span class="beat">models</span> Epoch AI corrects 42% of FrontierMath problems, doubling reported scores across the board

Released June 12, FrontierMath v2 corrects 123 problems in Tiers 1–3 and 12 in Tier 4, removing 5 and 7 respectively, leaving 338 total. The errors were categorized as "small but critical" — primarily cases where correct model answers were marked wrong — and affected 42% of the original benchmark. The practical consequence is severe: GPT-5.5's Tier 4 score nearly doubled from 35% to 72.5% once the grading was fixed, and the corrected leaderboard now shows Claude Fable 5 at approximately 88% on Tier 4 and 87% on Tiers 1–3. Any published capability comparison citing the original FrontierMath figures should be treated as unreliable; the corrected benchmark is the new baseline. [Epoch AI](https://epoch.ai/benchmarks/frontiermath-tiers-1-3-v2)

## <span class="beat">frameworks</span> Microsoft Agent Framework's CodeAct cuts agentic task overhead by half

At BUILD 2026 on June 3, Microsoft announced three production additions to MAF (which reached 1.0 GA in April as the converged AutoGen/Semantic Kernel platform). CodeAct is the most immediately useful: instead of separate tool-call roundtrips, the model generates a single Python program that invokes tools via `call_tool()`, yielding 52.4% faster execution and 63.9% fewer tokens on representative multi-step workloads. Foundry Hosted Agents handles deployment — scale-to-zero pricing, VM-isolated per-session sandboxes, and persistent filesystem state across scaling events — eliminating the ops work of turning a local agent into production infrastructure. Agent Harness adds automatic context compaction, session-scoped memory, and a BackgroundAgentsProvider for parallel subtask delegation. For teams already on the MAF stack, CodeAct is a drop-in latency and cost improvement that addresses the main production complaint about multi-step agents. [Microsoft Agent Framework blog](https://devblogs.microsoft.com/agent-framework/microsoft-agent-framework-at-build-2026-announce/)

## <span class="beat">oss</span> Zhipu releases GLM-5.2 under MIT with 1M-token context and order-of-magnitude cost advantage

Zhipu released GLM-5.2 on June 13 and published the full 753-billion-parameter weights under an MIT license three days later. The model uses a mixture-of-experts architecture with ~40B active parameters per token, a 1-million-token context window, and a new technique called IndexShare sparse attention — which reuses attention indices across sparse layers to keep long-context inference costs tractable. Third-party API pricing on platforms such as OpenRouter landed around $1.4 per million input tokens, roughly an order of magnitude below comparable frontier models. The release landed one day after the US suspended Fable 5 and Mythos 5 globally; Zhipu's announcement framed it as a statement that frontier intelligence should be freely available — and for enterprises worried about sovereign supply-chain risk in their model stack, a self-hostable MIT-licensed alternative at this capability level is now a credible option. [Eigent AI analysis](https://www.eigent.ai/blog/glm-5-2)

## <span class="beat">providers</span> OpenAI and Broadcom tape out Jalapeño, OpenAI's first custom inference ASIC

Announced June 24, Jalapeño is OpenAI's first custom chip: a reticle-sized inference ASIC co-designed with Broadcom and moved from initial design to manufacturing tape-out in nine months — a pace the companies describe as the fastest ASIC development cycle ever achieved at this complexity level. It targets inference specifically, not pretraining; engineering samples are running GPT-5.3-Codex-Spark at production target frequency and power with better performance-per-watt than current alternatives. Nvidia hardware will continue handling pretraining, but inference at scale is where OpenAI's per-query cost lives — and a production ASIC landing by end of 2026 gives OpenAI a direct lever on the unit economics that determine whether $20/month subscriptions and API pricing stay viable as usage scales toward gigawatt data center deployments with Microsoft. [OpenAI announcement](https://openai.com/index/openai-broadcom-jalapeno-inference-chip/) · [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/24/openai-unveils-its-first-custom-chip-built-by-broadcom/)

## <span class="beat">providers</span> Kimi K2.7 Code is the first open-weight model selectable in GitHub Copilot

Moonshot AI's Kimi K2.7 Code became generally available in the Copilot model picker on July 1. The full 1-trillion-parameter weights are public on Hugging Face; GitHub is running a hosted copy on Azure. For Business and Enterprise plans it is off by default — administrators must explicitly enable it before anyone in their organization can select it. Billing follows the usage-based AI Credits system GitHub introduced June 1, at provider list pricing. The enterprise governance implication is the notable part: open-weight models in the model picker change the audit posture entirely — an organization can inspect, fine-tune, or self-host the exact model underlying its coding assistant rather than treating the capability as a black-box API. [GitHub Changelog](https://github.blog/changelog/2026-07-01-kimi-k2-7-is-now-available-in-github-copilot/)

## <span class="beat">enterprise</span> Anthropic tells Senate that Alibaba ran 28.8 million Claude exchanges through 25,000 fake accounts

Anthropic's June 10 letter to the Senate Banking Committee alleged that Alibaba-affiliated operators ran 28.8 million exchanges with Claude models through approximately 25,000 fraudulent accounts between April 22 and June 5 — a campaign it characterized as the largest known AI distillation attack to date, 1.7× the combined February 2026 total from DeepSeek, Moonshot AI, and MiniMax. The claimed targets were Claude's highest-value commercial capabilities: software engineering, agentic reasoning, and long-horizon task completion. The letter became public on June 24; Senators Hagerty and Kim announced plans for a defense-bill amendment that would sanction Chinese firms found improperly extracting US model outputs to train competing systems. Alibaba denies the allegation. For enterprise AI buyers, this establishes model distillation as a category of IP risk that may drive new vendor due-diligence requirements — and potentially new API access controls — beyond what current terms of service enforce. [Digital Applied](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/anthropic-alibaba-distillation-campaign-2026-ai-ip-war)
